title: "Locking In"
date: 2026-07-15
---

I am nearing 3 years of being a professional software engineer. Within my short time in this field, watching AI make leaps and bounds within this industry is exciting but also very frightening.
I've been using AI as part of my normal day-to-day workflow for some time, though I realized the adverse effect it may be having on my ambitions to be exceptional within my field of work.
AI makes it easy to know practically anything... almost too easy. 

It dawned on me how reliant I've become on this tool and it's caused me to reflect on my level of proficiency as a professional.
Thus, I've made the decision to relax my usage of AI and ensure it is a last ditch effort on any solution going forward. I wish to struggle, research, architect, build, debug, and grow as a professional without prompting a computer to make decisions for me.
Nevertheless I am sure I will have points where I fall short or miss my mark, but I must regain the gratifying feeling of ME creating something.

The plan: Cancel AI subscriptions; research, build, troubleshoot the old fashion way in efforts to grow as professional and become exceptional in my field.
